## ğŸ¤” What is Cash Flow?

Imagine your business is like your personal wallet:

- **Cash Inflows** = Money **coming into** your wallet (like your salary, gifts received)
- **Cash Outflows** = Money **going out of** your wallet (like groceries, bills, rent)
- **Cash Flow** = The **difference** between what comes in and what goes out

**That's it!** Cash flow tracks the **actual money** moving in and out of your business bank account.

## ğŸ› ï¸ Practical Tips to Improve Cash Flow

### 1. Speed Up Cash Inflows (Get Money Faster)

**Problem**: Customers take 30-60 days to pay invoices
**Solution**:
- Offer 5% discount for immediate payment
- Accept card payments (instant cash)
- Send invoices same day, not next week
- Follow up on overdue payments weekly

**Result**: Get AED 10,000 today instead of waiting 2 months!

### 2. Slow Down Cash Outflows (Delay Payments Smartly)

**Problem**: You pay all suppliers immediately
**Solution**:
- Negotiate 30-day payment terms with suppliers
- Pay rent on the 5th, not the 1st (5 extra days)
- Batch salary payments (pay all staff same day)

**Result**: Keep AED 15,000 in the bank for 30 more days!

### 3. Reduce Unnecessary Spending

**Problem**: Spending on things you don't need
**Solution**:
- Review expense reports monthly
- Cancel unused subscriptions
- Buy supplies in bulk (cheaper)
- Track every AED spent

**Result**: Save AED 2,000/month = AED 24,000/year!

### 4. Build a Cash Buffer

**Problem**: One bad month and you can't pay rent
**Solution**:
- Save 10% of every sale in a "rainy day fund"
- Target: 3 months of expenses (AED 120,000)
- Don't touch this unless emergency

**Result**: Sleep better at night knowing you can survive 3 months without sales!


## ğŸ“š Glossary (Terms Explained Simply)

**Opening Balance**: Money in bank at start of month (like checking your wallet on Day 1)

**Cash Inflows**: All money received (customer payments, loans received)

**Cash Outflows**: All money paid out (rent, salaries, supplies)

**Net Cash Flow**: Difference between inflows and outflows (Inflows - Outflows)

**Closing Balance**: Money in bank at end of month (Opening + Net Cash Flow)

**GL Accounts**: Bank accounts tracked in the accounting system (Cash, Bank, Card Clearing)

**DR (Debit)**: Accounting term for money coming IN to cash accounts

**CR (Credit)**: Accounting term for money going OUT from cash accounts

**Liquidity**: How much cash you can access quickly (high = good)

**Cash Buffer**: Emergency savings to cover 2-3 months of expenses
